It will be noticed that each of these passages with one exception
(Ad. 969) contains a new proposal which one speaker places before
another in the form of a question. Under such circumstances it is
natural to inquire of another concerning his wish, but not concerning
his purpose. The latter will appear only when he is better informed.
This consideration renders the use of cupio in the answers to these
questions significant and appropriate.
